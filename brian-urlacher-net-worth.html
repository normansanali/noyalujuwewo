What is Brian Urlacher Net Worth?

Brian Urlacher, a former professional football linebacker, has a net worth of $16 million. He played 13 seasons with the Chicago Bears in the NFL and earned the nickname “Grrr-Lacher” for his outstanding defensive play.

Urlacher quickly became one of the team’s most valuable and popular players after joining the Bears in 2000. He earned the NFL Rookie of the Year Award in his first season. His other accomplishments include eight Pro Bowls, four first-team All-Pro honors, and the NFL Defensive Player of the Year award in 2005.

Urlacher played college football for the University of New Mexico before his NFL career. After retiring from athletic play in 2012, he became an analyst for Fox Sports. He was eventually inducted into the Pro Football Hall of Fame in 2018.

Early Life and College Years

Brian Urlacher was born in Pasco, Washington in 1978 and grew up with a younger brother. After his parents split up, he moved with his mother to Lovington, New Mexico where he played various sports including football, basketball, track, and table tennis. Despite his talent in multiple sports, Urlacher eventually focused on football and played as a running back, wide receiver, return specialist, and defensive back in high school. He attended the University of New Mexico where he studied criminology and played for the Lobos. During his college years, he was converted into a linebacker and later into a cross between a linebacker and free safety, receiving multiple honors and accolades for his performance.

Brian Urlacher’s NFL Career

Brian Urlacher was selected by the Chicago Bears during the first round of the 2000 NFL Draft, signing a five-year, $8-million contract with the team. He initially struggled and lost his starting position, but later showed his potential, eventually winning the 2000 Defensive Rookie of the Year award. Over the next few years, Urlacher continued to play an integral role for the Bears, winning the NFL Defensive Player of the Year award in 2005. However, after missing a large chunk of the 2009 season due to injury, Urlacher continued to struggle with various issues throughout the next few seasons, eventually announcing his retirement in 2013 via his Twitter account.

Controversy Surrounds Brian Urlacher’s Social Media Posts

Brian Urlacher, former Chicago Bears player, faced backlash in 2020 for his controversial social media posts. He criticized NBA players for boycotting games in response to the police shooting of a black man and also “liked” a post supporting shooting suspect Kyle Rittenhouse. Despite some support for his opinions, Urlacher’s actions received widespread criticism. The Chicago Bears issued a statement condemning his messages and distancing themselves from him.

Brian Urlacher’s Recent Real Estate Deals

In 2021, Brian Urlacher sold two significant properties within a few days. The first was his mansion in Queen Creek, Phoenix, which he listed for $2.5 million. The six-bedroom home with coffered ceilings and walls of glass spans 5,400 square feet and includes a gym, a guesthouse, and a pool. He originally bought the property for $780,000 in 2014 and sold it for a profit.
