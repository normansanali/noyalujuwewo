Who is Erykah Badu?

Erica Abi Wright known professionally as Erykah Badu was born on February 26, 1971, in Dallas, Texas. Her mother, Kolleen Maria Gipson, a beautician, raised her, and her father, William Wright Jr., a professional musician. Badu has an older sister, Nayrok, and a younger brother, Cody.

As a child, Badu grew up in a creative and spiritual environment. Her mother taught her to meditate and read tarot cards, while her father exposed her to various music genres. Badu began singing in a local Baptist church at the age of four.

At 14, she joined the Dallas Theater Center’s Junior Players, where she performed in various stage productions. Badu graduated from high school in 1988 and began studying theater at Grambling State University. She dropped out after one semester to pursue her music career.

In the early 1990s, Badu toured with her cousin, Robert Bradford, as part of the Ebony Aquarius group. In 1995, she moved to New York City and began working with producer D’Angelo on his debut album, Brown Sugar. The following year, she released her debut album, Baduizm, which featured the hit single “On & On.” Badu has since released five more studio albums and has won several Grammy Awards.
