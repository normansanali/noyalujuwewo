Who is Paul George?

Paul George, commonly referred to as “PG-13,” is an accomplished American basketball player who is presently an NBA player with the Los Angeles Clippers. George, who was born in Palmdale, California, on May 2, 1990, began playing basketball at Knight High School before going on to play for the Fresno State Bulldogs in college.

The Indiana Pacers drafted him with the tenth overall choice in the 2010 NBA draft, which launched a lucrative career. Paul George has accomplished several noteworthy accomplishments during his NBA career, such as being selected eight times as an NBA All-Star, being named to six All-NBA Teams, and being named to four NBA All-Defensive Teams.

Interestingly, in 2013 he was crowned the Most Improved Player in the NBA. George overcame setbacks, such as a fractured leg in 2014, to triumphantly return and win an Olympic gold medal in 2016.

Before joining the Los Angeles Clippers in 2019, he was a player for the Oklahoma City Thunder, where he demonstrated his flexibility as a top perimeter defender and scorer. Paul George, a versatile player renowned for his abilities and effect on the floor, stands 6 feet 8 inches tall.
