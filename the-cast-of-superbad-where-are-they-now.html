The Cast Of Superbad: Where Are They Now?

August 2024 Â· 6 minute read

Superbad was a coming-of-age comedy released in 2007 that has since become a staple film still watched and adored to this very day. The film starred some of Hollywood's biggest names including Seth Rogen, Jonah Hill, and Emma Stone, to name a few.

Related: The Truth About Jonah Hill And Seth Rogan's Relationship

The film itself launched the careers of its leading cast, so much that two Superbad stars have since gone on to become Academy-Award nominated actors. With the success of its cast, fans are now curious as to what their favorites have been up to today. While we know Seth Rogen went on to have a huge career in comedy, all while Michael Cera took a quieter path, what have the other cast members been up to since? Let's find out!

10 Jonah Hill

Jonah Hill, who co-wrote the hilarious film, played the role of Seth, who was loosely based on film co-writer, Seth Rogen in real life. While he had been known for his roles in movies such as The 40-Year-Old Virgin and Knocked Up, Superbad was easily the role that catapulted him to international fame and success.

Today, Jonah is not only an Academy-Award nominated actor but he's gone from in front of the camera to behind the scenes as a writer and director. The star wrote and directed the film Mid90s in 2018 and was the co-executive producer for the film, Beastie Boys Story

9 Michael Cera

Michael Cera played the role of Evan in Superbad, which was based on the film's other co-writer, Evan Goldberg. Prior to his time in the hilarious flick, Cera was widely known for his time on the hit Fox comedy series, Arrested Development.

While Cera has not been as active in the entertainment industry as many of his Superbad co-stars have been, he is still a successful TV and film actor. In addition to remaining part of the AD cast up until 2019, Michael landed roles in films such as Sausage Party, The Lego Batman Movie, and This Is The End, to name a few.

8 Seth Rogen

Seth Rogen took on the role of Officer Michaels, in which he acted alongside co-star, Bill Hader. The actor originally set out to play the role of Seth, however, by the time the film was made, Rogen figured he was too old. After appearing alongside Jonah Hill in the films The 40-Year-Old Virgin and Knocked Up, the duo reunited for the hit comedy film.

Seth went on to have an incredibly successful career in film starring in movies Pineapple Express, This Is The End and Bad Neighbors. In 2014, Seth wrote, produced, and directed The Interview, a film that went on to make headlines for its portrayal of North Korea. The actor's most recent role was in the live-action remake of The Lion King where he voiced none other than Pumba.

7 Christopher Mintz-Plasse

Christopher Mintz-Plasse played the iconic role of none other than Fogel, who went on to become widely known as his ID alias, McLovin'. This marked the actor's very first professional acting role, which catapulted him to stardom.

Related: The Awkward Thing Chris Mintz-Plasse Had To Do When Filming 'Superbad'

Following his time on Superbad, Mintz-Plasse went on to star in a number of films such as Role Models, Kick-Ass, Pitch-Perfect, and This Is The End. Christopher's most recent role was in the 2020 comedy-drama Promising Young Woman, in which he starred alongside big names such as Bo Burnham, Laverne Cox, and Carey Mulligan. Since then, fans have been wondering what Christopher has been up to, as he's yet to appear in anything since!

6 Bill Hader

Bill Hader played the role of Officer Slater opposite Seth Rogen in the hilarious flick. The actor first came to prominence as a cast member on NBC's Saturday Night Live all while appearing alongside Seth in Knocked Up.

Bill Hader went on to appear in a slew of successful films such as Inside Out, Finding Dory, and Trainwreck, in which he played the love interest to film lead and screenwriter, Amy Schumer. Most recently, Bill has appeared in the classic horror movie, It Chapter 2, and the Disney Plus holiday movie, Noelle.

5 Martha MacIsaac

Martha MacIsaac played the role of Becca, Evan's crush in the hit film. Prior to joining the cast of Superbad, the actress appeared on a handful of television shows and TV movies, including her role in the CBC drama, Emily Of New Moon.

The star is still very much involved with acting and has appeared in Unicorn Store, and What Keeps You Alive. Most recently, Martha landed roles on Greek, 1600 Penn, and the syndicated series, The Pinkertons, to name a few.

4 Emma Stone

Emma Stone played opposite Martha MacIsaac as Jules, Seth's crush. The film was Emma Stone's very first, as she had only worked on a number of TV shows prior. Following her time working alongside Michael Cera and Jonah Hill, Stone went on to appear in Easy A, Zombieland, and Crazy, Stupid, Love.

Today, the star is an Academy Award-winning actress for her role in La La Land. Emma is now set to take on the titular Disney role of Cruella de Vil in Cruella.

3 Dave Franco

Dave Franco may have only appeared in a single scene in the hit film, it remains the role that launched him to stardom. While his older bro, James Franco, was widely known at the time, Dave's role as one of Seth's classmates is one fans didn't forget.

Since then, Dave has become a household name appearing in films such as 21 Jump Street, Friday Night, and both the Bad Neighbors films alongside Seth Rogen. Today, Dave is married to fellow actress, Alison Brie, who is known for her roles in Community, and Mad Men.

2 Joe Lo Truglio

Joe Lo Truglio took on the role of Francis, a creepy driver who takes Seth and Evan to their house party! Lo Truglio was widely known prior to his time on Superbad, as he'd already appeared in The State and Wet Hot America Summer alongside David Wain, and Michael Ian Black.

The star later reunited with Seth Rogen in Pineapple Express before landing the role of Charles Boyle in the hit comedy series, Brooklyn Nine-Nine.

1 Kevin Corrigan

As Seth and Evan crash another house party to steal their booze, they come across Mark, played by none other than Kevin Corrigan. The actor is best know for his leading role as Uncle Eddie in the  Fox series, Grounded For Life.

In addition to his short, yet iconic role in Superbad, Kevin has taken on an array of gangster-esque roles throughout his career, landing himself roles in films such as The Departed, Bad Boys, Seven Psychopaths, and True Romance. Much like his fellow Superbad co-stars, Kevin reunited with Seth and Joe in the comedy, Pineapple Express.
