Michael Hartung And Jason Gotay’s Relationship

Michael Hartung, who is also an actor, met Jason Gotay on the set of the NBC live musical Peter Pan Live in 2014. And they started began dating soon after.

The couple announced their engagement in January 2021. Sharing photos of their engagement rings, on Instagram, Michael had ended the suspense regarding their love dynamic with a “GUESS WHAT. Surprising someone you’re around 24/7 is hard. BUT WORTH IT!”

However, those who followed them on social media certainly were aware of them expressing the likes of  — “Feeling extra lucky today. 5 years goes by fast when it’s with @jasongotay” or “The best four years” — every time the calendar marked 24th November. Of course, i.e, since 2014.

In August 2021, in an essay for Brides, Jason when detailing their love story had also revealed how they were planning the wedding of their dreams.

Just as they had planned, the two got married in a sophisticated, elegant, and beautiful backyard wedding at The Kester Homestead in Memphis, New York. Wedding planner Jason Mitchell made sure the bonfire and dancing under the stars-themed ceremonies were exactly to his clients’ expectations.

And now that they are married, Jason claims he is the happiest he has ever been.

That been said, as we write this, days after they exchanged I Dos on the 11th of October, Michael was yet to share his first picture with his husband.

Love Life star too had not shared glimpses from his big day after unveiling “I marry this man in 10 days” in an Instagram post on the 1st of October.

Michael Hartung Job

IMDB lists Michael Hartung as an actor, known for Love Life (2020), Fosse/Verdon (2019), and Love in the Underground: Side A (2020).

As per his profile on Facebook, he has been working at Barrington Stage Company in Pittsfield, Massachusetts since 2016.

Before that, at different times, he worked at Theatre Under The Stars in Houston, Texas, at The Gateway in Bellport, New York, at Music Theatre Wichita in Wichita, Kansas, at The Muny in Saint Louis, Missouri, and the West Virginia Public Theatre.

This way, through his career journey so far, Michael managed to save an estimated net worth of $400 thousand.

Speaking of his education, he studied Musical theatre at the University of Michigan, at the Royal Academy of Dramatic Art, and NYU Tisch School of the Arts.
